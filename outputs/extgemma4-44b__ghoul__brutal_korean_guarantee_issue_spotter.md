### I. Core Legal Issues to Investigate

**1. Nature of the Guarantee (연대보증 vs. 물상담보)**
The 2014 documents must be distinguished. A joint-and-several guarantee (연대보증) makes your father personally liable for the debt. A collateral agreement (물상담보) for equipment that no longer exists may have been extinguished if the equipment was legally disposed of or if the lender failed to maintain a security interest. The absence of the equipment does not automatically terminate a personal guarantee.

**2. Validity of Debt Acceleration (기한의 이익 상실)**
The 2019 letter may have declared the debt due in full (기한의 이익 상실). If valid, the entire principal plus accrued interest becomes payable immediately. However, if the acceleration was improper or if the lender failed to follow procedural requirements, the debt may still be considered as per the original installment schedule.

**3. Effect of Partial Payment and Acknowledgment (채무승인)**
The 2021 payment of 200,000 KRW by your uncle’s former employee is critical. Under Korean law, a payment made by a third party can constitute an acknowledgment of the debt (채무승인) by the principal debtor if the debtor authorized or ratified it. This could restart the statute of limitations (which is 5 years for commercial claims, but the nature of the guarantee matters). The 2023 text message claiming "your family already acknowledged this" suggests the lender is relying on this payment as a waiver of any statute of limitations defense.

**4. Inheritance and Estate Liability (상속채무)**
Upon your father's death in 2022, his debts pass to his heirs. Your mother, as a surviving spouse, may be liable unless she filed a renunciation of inheritance (상속포기) or a limited acceptance (한정승인) within three months of his death. The "estate paperwork" you found must be scrutinized to determine if these filings were made or missed. If missed, she may be liable for the full debt, or if she accepted the estate, her liability is limited to the assets she inherited.

**5. Assignment of Claim (채권양도)**
The lender assigned the claim twice. The validity of these assignments, and whether proper notice was given to the debtor (your father) and his estate, affects who has the legal right to collect. A flawed assignment could render the current collection company's demands invalid.
