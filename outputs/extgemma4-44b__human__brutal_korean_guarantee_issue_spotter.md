The first thing we must do is separate the two documents from 2014. You say one is a joint-and-several guarantee (연대보증), and the other may be a collateral agreement for equipment that no longer exists. This is a critical distinction.

### II. The Assignment Chain: The "Double" Assignment

You say the lender assigned the claim twice—first to a savings bank affiliate, then to a collection company. This is a dangerous area. Under Korean law, an assignment of a claim (채권양도) must be properly notified to the debtor.

**The Trap:** If the first assignment was not properly notified, or if the second assignment was made without the first assignee’s consent, the second assignment may be invalid. The collection company may be acting without legal standing. Furthermore, if the claim was assigned twice in full, the lender may have committed a fraudulent act, or at least an error that benefits you.

**What to investigate:**
*   **The assignment agreements:** Were they *채권양도계약* (claim assignment contracts) or *채무인수* (assumption of debt)?
*   **The notifications:** Was a *채권양도통지* (notice of assignment) sent to your father (or his estate) for both assignments? When? By what method?
*   **The sequence:** Did the savings bank actually receive the claim, or was it a paper assignment that never left the original lender’s control?

The collector’s 2023 text saying “your family already acknowledged this” likely refers to this payment.

### IV. The Estate and the Inheritance Issues

Your father passed in 2022. Your mother received calls in 2026. You are unsure about inheritance renunciation or limited acceptance.

**The Trap:** Under the Civil Code, an heir has three choices: simple acceptance, limited acceptance (한정승인), or renunciation (상속포기). The deadline is three months from the date the heir becomes aware of the inheritance. If your mother did not act, she may have simply accepted the estate, including the debts. However, if she was not properly notified of the debt, or if the debt was not part of the estate, she may not be liable.

**What to investigate:**
*   **The inheritance documents:** Look for any *상속포기서* (renunciation of inheritance) or *한정승인신고서* (report of limited acceptance).
*   **The timeline:** When did your mother become aware of the debt? The calls in 2026 are the first time she heard, but the law cares about when she *could* have known.
