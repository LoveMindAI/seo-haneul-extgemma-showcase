### I. The Primary Legal Issues (The Mechanisms)

**1. The Validity and Nature of the Guarantee (연대보증 - Joint and Several Guarantee)**
We must determine if the 2014 document was a *Yeondae-bojeung*. In Korea, this is a heavy burden; the creditor can demand full payment from the guarantor without first attempting to collect from the primary debtor. We must check if the guarantee had a fixed term or if it was indefinite. If it was indefinite, we look for the "statute of limitations" (소멸시효).

**2. The Chain of Assignment (채권양도 - Assignment of Claims)**
The debt moved twice. For an assignment to be valid against the debtor, the original creditor must have notified the debtor or the debtor must have consented. If the notification for the first or second transfer was flawed, the collection company may have no legal standing to demand payment.

**3. The Interruption of the Statute of Limitations (시효중단)**
This is the most dangerous part of your timeline. A commercial claim typically expires in five years. 
*   **The 2019 Letter:** Did this "accelerate" the debt (기한의 이익 상실), or did it serve as a formal demand that reset the five-year clock?
*   **The 2021 Payment:** A payment by a third party (the employee) can be interpreted as an "acknowledgment of debt" (채무승인). If the law views this as your father's debt being acknowledged, the clock reset in 2021.

**4. The Status of the Estate (상속 - Inheritance)**
Your father passed in 2022. Under Korean law, debts are inherited along with assets. 
*   **Qualified Acceptance (한정승인):** Did your mother or you accept the inheritance only to the extent of the assets inherited?
*   **Renunciation (상속포기):** Did anyone formally renounce the inheritance within three months of knowing of the death?
If neither was done, the debt may have transferred to the heirs in full.
