### 1. Primary Legal Issues to Investigate

**A. The Nature of the Obligation (The "Guarantee")**
We must determine if the document signed in 2014 is a *Yeondae-bojeung* (연대보증 - Joint and Several Guarantee). In Korea, this is a rigorous obligation where the guarantor is treated as a primary debtor; the lender does not have to exhaust options against the uncle before demanding payment from the father.
*   **Issue:** Was the guarantee limited in amount or duration? Was it a "blanket" guarantee?

**B. The Validity of the Collateral (The Equipment)**
If the equipment no longer exists, we must check if the collateral was a *Yangdo-dambo* (양도담보 - Transfer of Title for Security).
*   **Issue:** If the collateral vanished, did the lender lose their security interest, or does the debt simply revert to an unsecured claim?

**C. The Chain of Assignment (The "Paper Trail")**
The claim moved from the original lender $\rightarrow$ Savings Bank $\rightarrow$ Collection Company.
*   **Issue:** Was the *Chae-gwon-yang-do* (채권양도 - Assignment of Claim) properly notified to the debtor (your father) via certified mail? Under Korean law, if the debtor was not notified of the assignment, the new creditor may lack the legal standing to demand payment.

**D. Statute of Limitations (Somyul-sihyo - 소멸시효)**
Commercial debts in Korea typically have a shorter statute of limitations (usually 5 years) than general civil debts (10 years).
*   **Issue:** Did the debt expire between 2014 and 2026?
*   **The "Trap":** The 2019 acceleration letter and the 2021 payment are critical. A payment—even a small one—can act as an "acknowledgment of debt" (*Chae-mu-ing-sik*), which resets the statute of limitations clock.

**E. Inheritance and Liability (Sang-sok - 상속)**
Your father died in 2022. Debt is inherited along with assets.
*   **Issue:** Did the heirs perform *Sang-sok-pogi* (상속포기 - Renunciation of Inheritance) or *Han-jeong-seung-in* (한정승인 - Limited Acceptance)?
*   **The Deadline:** These must generally be filed within 3 months of knowing about the death/debt. If the mother only learned of the debt in 2026, she may be eligible for "Special Limited Acceptance" (*Teuk-byeol-han-jeong-seung-in*).

### 2. The "Traps" in the Facts

*   **The 2021 Payment:** You mentioned a former employee of your uncle paid 200,000 KRW. If the lender argues this payment was made *on behalf of the estate* or *by the father's agent*, they will claim the statute of limitations was reset. We must investigate if this employee had any legal authority to bind your father's estate.
*   **The 2023 Text Message:** The phrase "your family already acknowledged this" is a common tactic used by collection agencies to induce a "voluntary" admission of debt. If a family member replied "I'm sorry" or "We will try to pay," the collector may argue this constitutes a new acknowledgment of the debt by the heirs.
*   **The Mother's Status:** The mother is being contacted, but she did not sign the documents. Unless she is an heir who accepted the estate, she is not personally liable. The collectors are likely attempting to pressure her into "voluntarily" assuming the debt.

### 3. What Nobody Should Assume Yet

1.  **Do not assume the debt is valid.** The chain of assignment may be broken.
2.  **Do not assume the 2021 payment resets the clock.** A third party's payment does not always constitute a legal acknowledgment by the debtor.
3.  **Do not assume the inheritance deadline is missed.** The "Special Limited Acceptance" exists specifically for debts discovered years after death.
4.  **Do not assume the "Joint and Several" status.** Some documents look like guarantees but are actually "letters of intent" or "comfort letters" which carry different legal weights.
